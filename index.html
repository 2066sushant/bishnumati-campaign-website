About the Campaign

The Bishnumati Cleaning Campaign is a dedicated community initiative launched in **2071 BS (Bikram Sambat)**, focused on the revitalization and preservation of the Bishnumati River in Nepal. Our mission extends beyond merely cleaning the river; we strive to foster a sustainable environment by engaging local communities in various conservation efforts.

The campaign was born out of a deep concern for the ecological degradation of the Bishnumati River, a vital waterway that flows through the heart of Kathmandu. Over the years, it has faced significant pollution challenges due to rapid urbanization and insufficient waste management. We believe that a clean river is not just an aesthetic improvement but a crucial step towards better public health and ecological balance.

Our Core Activities

Our campaign employs a multifaceted approach to achieve its goals, focusing on hands-on cleaning, ecological restoration, and public education:

  • **River Cleaning Drives:** Regular clean-up operations along the Bishnumati riverbanks, removing solid waste, plastics, and debris.
  • **Plantation Programs:** Planting trees and vegetation along the river to stabilize banks, improve air quality, and enhance biodiversity.
  • **Community Awareness Programs:** Conducting workshops, seminars, and street plays to educate locals about the importance of river cleanliness, waste segregation, and responsible disposal.
  • **Tree Conservation:** Promoting the protection and growth of existing trees and discouraging deforestation in the river's vicinity.
  • **Public Health Advocacy:** Highlighting the link between a clean environment and public health, encouraging hygienic practices.

Impact & Photo Gallery

Since its inception in 2071 BS, the Bishnumati Cleaning Campaign has made significant strides in improving the river's condition and fostering environmental consciousness. Our efforts have led to:

  • Noticeable reduction in visible pollution along designated stretches.
  • Increased green cover through successful tree plantation drives.
  • Greater community participation and a growing sense of environmental responsibility.
  • Improved awareness among younger generations about conservation.
